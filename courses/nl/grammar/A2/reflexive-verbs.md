# Reflexive verbs

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 26  
**Depends on:** [Object pronouns](object-pronouns.md)

---

## Explanation

A **reflexive verb** (*reflexief werkwoord*) is one where the subject and object refer to the same person — the action bounces back to the doer. Dutch marks this with a **reflexive pronoun** (*zich* or a personal form):

> *Hij wast **zich**.* — He washes himself.

Many Dutch reflexive verbs have non-reflexive English equivalents, so you cannot always translate *zich* literally.

---

## The reflexive pronouns

| Subject | Reflexive | English |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| ik | **me** (unstressed) / **mezelf** (stressed) | myself |
| jij/je | **je** (unstressed) / **jezelf** (stressed) | yourself |
| u | **zich** / **uzelf** | yourself (formal) |
| hij/zij/het | **zich** | himself / herself / itself |
| wij/we | **ons** / **onszelf** | ourselves |
| jullie | **je** / **jezelf** | yourselves |
| zij/ze (pl.) | **zich** | themselves |

> **zich** covers *u, hij, zij, het* (singular) and *zij* (plural). For *ik, jij, wij, jullie*, the reflexive is the same as the unstressed object pronoun (*me, je, ons, je*).

## Common reflexive verbs

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **zich voelen** | to feel (a certain way) |
| **zich herinneren** | to remember |
| **zich vergissen** | to be mistaken / make an error |
| **zich voorstellen** | to introduce oneself / to imagine |
| **zich gedragen** | to behave |
| **zich bevinden** | to be located (formal) |
| **zich bezighouden met** | to occupy oneself with / to deal with |
| **zich aankleden** | to get dressed |
| **zich wassen** | to wash (oneself) |
| **zich scheren** | to shave |
| **zich haasten** | to hurry |
| **zich realiseren** | to realise |
| **zich zorgen maken** | to worry |
| **zich afvragen** | to wonder |

---

## Examples with conjugation

### zich voelen (to feel)

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik voel **me** goed. | I feel well. |
| Jij voelt **je** ziek. | You feel ill. |
| Hij voelt **zich** moe. | He feels tired. |
| Wij voelen **ons** thuis. | We feel at home. |
| Ze voelen **zich** welkom. | They feel welcome. |

### zich vergissen (to be mistaken)

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik vergis **me**. | I am mistaken. |
| Ze vergist **zich** vaak. | She is often mistaken. |
| U vergist **zich**. | You are mistaken. *(formal)* |

---

## Stressed reflexives: *zichzelf, mezelf, jezelf* …

When the reflexive is emphasised — especially in contrast — use the stressed *-zelf* form:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Hij doet het **zelf**. | He does it himself. |
| Ze wast **zichzelf**. | She washes herself. *(emphasis)* |
| Vergeet **jezelf** niet. | Don't forget yourself. |
| We redden **onszelf** wel. | We'll manage ourselves. |

---

## Reflexive vs non-reflexive

Some verbs exist in both reflexive and non-reflexive forms with different meanings:

| Non-reflexive | Reflexive |
|--------------|-----------|
| *Hij gedraagt de kinderen.* — He controls the children. | *Hij gedraagt **zich** goed.* — He behaves well. |
| *Ze stelt hem voor.* — She introduces him. | *Ze stelt **zich** voor.* — She introduces herself. |
| *Ik herinner hem eraan.* — I remind him of it. | *Ik herinner **me** het goed.* — I remember it well. |

---

## Examples in context

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik voel **me** vandaag niet goed. | I don't feel well today. |
| Hij herinnert **zich** haar naam niet meer. | He no longer remembers her name. |
| Ze haast **zich** om de bus te halen. | She hurries to catch the bus. |
| Stelt u **zich** even voor? | Could you introduce yourself? *(formal)* |
| Jullie vergissen **je** — dit is niet het goede adres. | You are mistaken — this is not the right address. |
| We maken **ons** zorgen over het weer. | We are worried about the weather. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *zich* for *ik* or *wij***

*Ik voel **zich** goed.* ✗ → *Ik voel **me** goed.* ✓  
*Wij vergissen **zich**.* ✗ → *Wij vergissen **ons**.* ✓

**2. Omitting the reflexive pronoun entirely**

*Hij voelt goed.* ✗ (ungrammatical without object) → *Hij voelt **zich** goed.* ✓

**3. Confusing reflexive with object pronoun**

The reflexive is used when subject = object. When they differ, use the regular object pronoun:  
*Ik was mij.* (reflexive: I wash myself) vs *Ik was hem.* (I wash him — someone else)

## Quick reference

```
ik      → me  / mezelf
jij/je  → je  / jezelf
u       → zich / uzelf
hij/zij/het → zich / zichzelf
wij     → ons / onszelf
jullie  → je  / jezelf
zij     → zich / zichzelf

Common verbs: zich voelen, zich herinneren, zich vergissen,
              zich voorstellen, zich haasten, zich gedragen,
              zich afvragen, zich zorgen maken
```
