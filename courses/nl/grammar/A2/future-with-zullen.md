# Future with zullen

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 28  
**Depends on:** [Future with gaan](../A1/future-with-gaan.md) | [Main clause word order](../A1/word-order.md)

---

## Explanation

Dutch has three ways to express the future. You already know two: *gaan + infinitive* and the simple present. **Zullen + infinitive** is the third, and it carries a different nuance — it emphasises **certainty, promise, or prediction** rather than personal intention.

| Construction | Best for | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **present tense** | scheduled / fixed events | *De trein vertrekt om tien uur.* |
| **gaan + inf.** | personal plans / intentions | *Ik ga morgen werken.* |
| **zullen + inf.** | promises, predictions, formal future | *Het zal morgen regenen.* |

---

## Conjugation of *zullen*

| Subject | Form |
|---------|------|
| ik | **zal** |
| jij/je | **zult** / **zal** |
| u | **zult** |
| hij/zij/het | **zal** |
| wij/we | **zullen** |
| jullie | **zullen** |
| zij/ze (pl.) | **zullen** |

> *zult* and *zal* are both correct for *jij*. *Zal jij…?* is the inverted form (no *t*-drop because the form used is *zal*).

---

## Word order: infinitive at the end

The same rule as modal verbs — *zullen* occupies position 2, infinitive goes to the end:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik **zal** het morgen **doen**. | I will do it tomorrow. |
| Ze **zullen** ons **helpen**. | They will help us. |
| **Zal** het morgen **regenen**? | Will it rain tomorrow? |

---

## When to use *zullen*

### Promises and commitments

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik **zal** je bellen. | I will call you. *(promise)* |
| We **zullen** er zijn. | We will be there. |
| Hij **zal** het niet vergeten. | He will not forget it. |

### Predictions and expectations

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Het **zal** morgen regenen. | It will rain tomorrow. |
| Dat **zal** wel lukken. | That will probably work out. |
| Ze **zullen** het begrijpen. | They will understand it. |

### Formal or written future

In formal writing and speech, *zullen* is preferred over *gaan* for any future reference:

> *De vergadering **zal** plaatsvinden op dinsdag.* — The meeting will take place on Tuesday.

### Polite offers and suggestions

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Zal** ik de deur opendoen? | Shall I open the door? |
| **Zullen** we gaan? | Shall we go? |
| **Zal** ik dat voor je doen? | Shall I do that for you? |

---

## *zullen* vs *gaan* — the key contrast

| | *gaan* | *zullen* |
|--|--------|---------|
| Focus | intention, personal plan | certainty, prediction, promise |
| Register | everyday speech | slightly more formal |
| Example | *Ik ga hem bellen.* | *Ik zal hem bellen.* |
| Nuance | I'm planning to call him | I will call him (I promise) |

In practice the boundary is fluid, and *zullen* is often heard in spoken Dutch for promises and offers.

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik **zal** het niet meer doen. | I won't do it again. *(promise)* |
| **Zal** ik een kopje thee **zetten**? | Shall I make a cup of tea? |
| Het **zal** druk zijn op de weg. | It will be busy on the road. *(prediction)* |
| **Zullen** we dit weekend **gaan fietsen**? | Shall we go cycling this weekend? |
| Ze **zullen** binnenkort **aankomen**. | They will arrive shortly. |
| Dat **zal** wel zo zijn. | That will probably be the case. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *zullen* for a personal plan (overly formal)**

*Ik zal morgen boodschappen doen.* — grammatically correct but sounds stiff for casual speech; *Ik ga morgen boodschappen doen* is more natural.

**2. Placing the infinitive in the middle**

*Ik zal doen het morgen.* ✗ → *Ik zal het morgen **doen**.* ✓

**3. Conjugating the infinitive**

*Hij zal gaat.* ✗ → *Hij zal **gaan**.* ✓

---

## Quick reference

```
zullen:  ik zal / jij zult (zal) / hij zal / wij zullen

Use for:  promises, predictions, formal future, offers (Zal ik …?)
Contrast: gaan = intention/plan; present = scheduled; zullen = certainty/promise

Word order:  [zullen (pos.2)]  [rest]  [infinitive (end)]
```
