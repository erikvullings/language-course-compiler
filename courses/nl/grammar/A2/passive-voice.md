# Passive voice

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 30  
**Depends on:** [Perfect tense](../A1/perfect-tense.md) | [Main clause word order](../A1/word-order.md)

---

## Explanation

The **passive voice** (*lijdende vorm*) shifts focus from the actor (agent) to the action or its receiver. Dutch has two passive constructions — a *worden* passive for ongoing/current actions and a *zijn* passive for states that result from a completed action.

```
Active:   De kok maakt de soep.         The cook makes the soup.
Passive:  De soep wordt gemaakt.        The soup is being made.
State:    De soep is gemaakt.           The soup is made (ready).
```

---

## The *worden* passive — present and past

Used for actions in progress or processes:

```
[Subject]  +  worden (conjugated)  +  [rest]  +  past participle
De soep       wordt                             gemaakt.
```

### Present passive

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| De brief **wordt geschreven**. | The letter is being written. |
| De auto **wordt gerepareerd**. | The car is being repaired. |
| De vergadering **wordt gehouden** in Utrecht. | The meeting is being held in Utrecht. |

### Simple past passive

*Worden* → *werd* (singular) / *werden* (plural):

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| De brief **werd geschreven** door Marie. | The letter was written by Marie. |
| De huizen **werden gebouwd** in 1920. | The houses were built in 1920. |

### Perfect passive

*Worden* in the perfect uses *zijn* as auxiliary + *geworden* as past participle — but in the passive the *ge-* of *geworden* is dropped, leaving just *worden*:

> *De brief **is geschreven**.* — The letter has been written. *(see zijn-passive below)*

---

## The *zijn* passive — resultant state

Used for the state that results from a completed action:

| *worden* (process) | *zijn* (result state) |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| De deur **wordt** gesloten. *(being closed)* | De deur **is** gesloten. *(is/was closed — it is shut now)* |
| Het raam **werd** gebroken. *(was broken in the act)* | Het raam **is** gebroken. *(the window is broken)* |

---

## Optional agent with *door*

To express who performed the action, add *door* (by):

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| De brief werd geschreven **door** Marie. | The letter was written **by** Marie. |
| De auto is gerepareerd **door** een monteur. | The car has been repaired **by** a mechanic. |
| Het huis werd gebouwd **door** een lokale aannemer. | The house was built **by** a local contractor. |

> The agent is optional; in many passive sentences the agent is unknown or unimportant.

---

## Conjugation of *worden*

| Subject | Present | Simple past |
|---------|---------|------------|
| ik | **word** | **werd** |
| jij/je | **wordt** | **werd** |
| u | **wordt** | **werd** |
| hij/zij/het | **wordt** | **werd** |
| wij/we | **worden** | **werden** |
| jullie | **worden** | **werden** |
| zij/ze (pl.) | **worden** | **werden** |

---

## Word order in passive sentences

Same rules as active sentences — the conjugated auxiliary (*worden* or *zijn*) is in position 2; the past participle goes to the end:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Vandaag **wordt** het rapport **gepresenteerd**. | Today the report is being presented. |
| **Wordt** het probleem **opgelost**? | Is the problem being solved? |
| Hier **wordt** niet **gerookt**. | Smoking is not permitted here. *(lit. is not smoked)* |

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Het museum **wordt** gerestaureerd. | The museum is being restored. |
| Die film **werd** gemaakt in 1985. | That film was made in 1985. |
| Is de pizza al **besteld**? | Has the pizza been ordered yet? |
| De kinderen **worden** naar school gebracht. | The children are being taken to school. |
| De brief **is** al verstuurd. | The letter has already been sent. |
| Hier **wordt** Nederlands **gesproken**. | Dutch is spoken here. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *zijn* for an ongoing process**

*De auto is gerepareerd worden.* ✗ → *De auto **wordt** gerepareerd.* ✓ (ongoing)  
or *De auto **is** gerepareerd.* ✓ (already done)

**2. Wrong agent preposition**

*door hem geschreven* ✓ — use *door* for agent, not *van* or *met*.

**3. Forgetting to move the participle to the end**

*De soep wordt gemaakt niet.* ✗ → *De soep wordt niet **gemaakt**.* ✓

**4. Using *worden* past participle in perfect passive**

*De brief is geworden geschreven.* ✗ → *De brief **is geschreven**.* ✓ (*worden* in the passive perfect is suppressed)

---

## Quick reference

```
Process (worden):
  Present:     subject + wordt/worden + [rest] + participle
  Simple past: subject + werd/werden  + [rest] + participle

Result state (zijn):
  subject + is/zijn + [rest] + participle

Agent:  door + noun/pronoun (optional)

worden conjugation (present): word / wordt / wordt / worden / worden / worden
worden conjugation (past):    werd / werd / werd / werden / werden / werden
```
