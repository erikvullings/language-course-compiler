# om … te + infinitive

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 25  
**Depends on:** [Subordinate clauses](subordinate-clauses.md) | [Modal verbs](../A1/modal-verbs.md)

---

## Explanation

The construction **om + te + infinitive** expresses **purpose** (in order to), **result** or **content** after certain adjectives and verbs. The infinitive always goes to the **end**; *te* is placed immediately before it.

```
Ik ga naar de winkel  om  brood  te kopen.
I go to the shop      in order  bread  to buy.
```

---

## The three main uses

### 1. Purpose — *in order to*

The most common use: explaining why someone does something.

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ze studeert hard **om** te **slagen**. | She studies hard in order to pass. |
| Hij spaart **om** een auto te **kopen**. | He is saving up to buy a car. |
| We gaan vroeg weg **om** de file te **vermijden**. | We leave early to avoid the traffic jam. |

> *om* can be omitted when the purpose is very clear and short, but including it is always correct.

### 2. After adjectives — *te* + infinitive (without *om*)

After adjectives with *zijn*, *te* + infinitive is used **without** *om*:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Het is moeilijk **te begrijpen**. | It is difficult to understand. |
| Ze is blij **te horen** dat je komt. | She is happy to hear that you're coming. |
| Het is fijn **hier te zijn**. | It is nice to be here. |

### 3. After verbs + *om* — content infinitive

Certain verbs are followed by *om … te*:

| Verb | Example | English |
|------|---------|---------|
| **proberen** | Ik probeer **om** vroeg op **te staan**. | I try to get up early. |
| **vergeten** | Ze is vergeten **om** te **bellen**. | She forgot to call. |
| **besluiten** | Hij besloot **om** te **gaan**. | He decided to go. |
| **beginnen** | We beginnen **om** te **eten**. | We start eating. |
| **vragen** | Ze vroeg **om** te **komen**. | She asked to come. |

> With *proberen, vergeten, besluiten*, the *om* is often dropped in informal speech: *Ik probeer vroeg op te staan.* Both forms are correct.

---

## Separable verbs: *te* inserts between prefix and stem

For separable verbs, *te* goes **between** the prefix and the infinitive:

| Separable verb | om … te form |
|---------------|-------------|
| opstaan | om op **te** staan |
| afwassen | om af **te** wassen |
| meenemen | om mee **te** nemen |
| thuiskomen | om thuis **te** komen |

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Hij staat vroeg op **om** op tijd **te vertrekken**. | He gets up early in order to leave on time. |
| Ze koopt boodschappen **om** voor haar moeder **te koken**. | She buys groceries to cook for her mother. |

---

## *om … te* vs *dat*-clause

| *om … te* | *dat*-clause |
|-----------|-------------|
| same subject in both clauses | different subjects possible |
| Ik ga **om te leren**. | Ik hoop **dat jij leert**. |
| She studies to pass. | She hopes that he passes. |

When the subject of the purpose/content clause is the **same** as the main clause subject, use *om … te*. When subjects differ, use a *dat*-clause.

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Hij belt haar **om** haar te **bedanken**. | He calls her to thank her. |
| Ze werkt hard **om** goed te **leven**. | She works hard to live well. |
| Het is te moeilijk **om te begrijpen**. | It is too difficult to understand. |
| We moeten vertrekken **om** op tijd **aan te komen**. | We have to leave in order to arrive on time. |
| Ik heb geen tijd **om te wachten**. | I have no time to wait. |
| Hij vergat **om** zijn sleutels **mee te nemen**. | He forgot to take his keys along. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Wrong position of *te***

*om te brood kopen* ✗ → *om brood **te kopen*** ✓  
*te* goes immediately before the infinitive, after all other elements.

**2. Separable verb: forgetting to split *te***

*om opstaan* ✗ → *om op **te** staan* ✓

**3. Using *om … te* when subjects differ**

*Ik hoop om jij te komen.* ✗ → *Ik hoop **dat jij komt**.* ✓

---

## Quick reference

```
Purpose:         [main clause]  om  [rest]  te + infinitive
Separable:       om  [prefix]  te  [stem]   (om op te staan)
te without om:   after adjectives with zijn  (moeilijk te begrijpen)
With verbs:      proberen / vergeten / besluiten / beginnen  om te …

Same subject → om … te
Different subjects → dat-clause
```
