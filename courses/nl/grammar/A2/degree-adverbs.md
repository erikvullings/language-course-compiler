# Degree adverbs

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 33  
**Depends on:** [Attributive adjectives](../A1/adjectives.md) | [Comparative and superlative](../A1/comparative-superlative.md)

---

## Explanation

**Degree adverbs** (*graadadverbia*) modify adjectives and other adverbs to express how strongly a quality applies — from *too much* down to *a little bit*. They always come **directly before** the word they modify and never inflect.

---

## Intensifiers — very / extremely

| Dutch | English | Register |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **heel** | very | neutral, common |
| **erg** | very / awfully | neutral, common |
| **zeer** | very | slightly formal / written |
| **enorm** | enormously | emphatic |
| **ontzettend** | terribly / incredibly | emphatic, spoken |
| **verschrikkelijk** | terribly | emphatic |
| **super** | super | informal |
| **hartstikke** | really / dead | informal, colloquial |

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Het is **heel** lekker. | It is very tasty. |
| Dat is **erg** moeilijk. | That is very difficult. |
| Ze is **ontzettend** aardig. | She is incredibly nice. |
| Het was **enorm** druk. | It was enormously busy. |

> **heel** vs **erg**: both mean *very*, but *heel* tends to be used with positive or neutral adjectives, *erg* with negative ones — though in practice both are interchangeable.

---

## Mid-range — fairly / rather / quite

| Dutch | English | Register |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **vrij** | fairly / rather | neutral |
| **tamelijk** | fairly / rather | slightly formal |
| **nogal** | rather / quite | often implies slightly negative |
| **behoorlijk** | quite / considerably | neutral |
| **redelijk** | reasonably / fairly | neutral |

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Het is **vrij** duur. | It is fairly expensive. |
| Ze is **tamelijk** tevreden. | She is fairly satisfied. |
| Het was **nogal** vervelend. | It was rather annoying. |
| Hij is **behoorlijk** goed. | He is quite good. |

---

## Downtoners — a bit / slightly

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **een beetje** | a bit / a little |
| **wat** | somewhat / a bit *(informal)* |
| **enigszins** | somewhat *(formal)* |
| **lichtjes** / **licht** | slightly *(formal)* |

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik ben **een beetje** moe. | I am a bit tired. |
| Hij is **wat** nerveus. | He is somewhat nervous. |
| Ze voelde zich **enigszins** teleurgesteld. | She felt somewhat disappointed. |

---

## Limit words — too / enough / almost

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **te** | too |
| **genoeg** | enough |
| **bijna** | almost / nearly |
| **net** | just / barely |
| **nauwelijks** | barely / scarcely |

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Het is **te** duur. | It is too expensive. |
| Is het warm **genoeg**? | Is it warm enough? |
| Ze is **bijna** klaar. | She is almost done. |
| Hij reed **net** te hard. | He drove just a bit too fast. |

> **genoeg** comes **after** the adjective or adverb it modifies: *groot genoeg* (big enough), never *genoeg groot*.

---

## *te* vs *zo … dat*

*Te* means *too* (excessively). The consequence can be expressed with *om … te* or *zo … dat*:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Het is **te** zwaar **om te tillen**. | It is too heavy to lift. |
| Het is **zo** zwaar **dat** niemand het kan tillen. | It is so heavy that nobody can lift it. |

---

## Position of degree adverbs

Degree adverbs always precede the adjective or adverb they modify:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **heel** goed | very good |
| **te** laat | too late |
| **bijna** altijd | almost always |
| **nog** niet **genoeg** | still not enough |

In predicative position: *Het is **heel** mooi.* ✓  
In attributive position (with adjective -e ending still applies to the adjective): *een **heel** mooie dag* ✓

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| De soep is **heel** lekker maar **een beetje** te zout. | The soup is very tasty but a bit too salty. |
| Hij loopt **vrij** langzaam voor een atleet. | He walks fairly slowly for an athlete. |
| Ze is **bijna** klaar met haar huiswerk. | She is almost done with her homework. |
| Het was **nogal** koud voor de tijd van het jaar. | It was rather cold for the time of year. |
| Die tas is **genoeg** groot voor al mijn spullen. | That bag is big enough for all my things. |
| De muziek staat **te** hard. | The music is too loud. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Placing *genoeg* before the adjective**

*genoeg groot* ✗ → *groot **genoeg*** ✓

**2. Confusing *te* (too) with *erg/heel* (very)**

*Het is te lekker!* — colloquially means *it's incredibly tasty* (used as an intensifier in informal speech), but literally means *it's too tasty*, which is illogical. In formal usage, use *heel lekker*.

**3. Using *zo* when you mean *heel***

*Het is zo duur.* can mean *it's so expensive* (emotional) or introduce a *dat*-consequence. For plain *very*, use *heel* or *erg*.

---

## Quick reference

```
Intensifiers:  heel / erg / zeer / ontzettend / enorm
Mid-range:     vrij / tamelijk / nogal / behoorlijk / redelijk
Downtoners:    een beetje / wat / enigszins
Limit words:   te (too) / genoeg (enough, AFTER adj.) / bijna (almost)

Position: always directly before the modified word
Exception: genoeg comes AFTER the adjective
```
