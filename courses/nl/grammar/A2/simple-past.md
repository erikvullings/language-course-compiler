# Simple past (weak verbs)

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 19  
**Depends on:** [Present tense](../A1/present-tense.md) | [Perfect tense](../A1/perfect-tense.md)  
**Signal words:** *vroeger, toen, altijd (habitual past), ooit*

---

## Explanation

Dutch has two simple past tenses. You already know the **perfect** (*voltooid tegenwoordige tijd*), which is the default past tense in spoken Dutch. The **simple past** (*onvoltooid verleden tijd*, also called the *imperfectum*) is used mainly in:

- **written** Dutch (stories, news, formal prose)
- **spoken** Dutch for habitual or background past states, especially with *zijn*, *hebben*, and modal verbs
- narration where a sequence of events flows naturally

For most verbs in conversation, the perfect and the simple past are interchangeable. But *was/waren*, *had/hadden*, *kon*, *mocht*, *moest*, *wilde*, and *zou* are almost always used in their simple past forms even in speech.

---

## Forming the simple past of weak (regular) verbs

### Step 1 — find the stem

Same as for the present tense: infinitive minus *-en*.

### Step 2 — apply the *'t kofschip* rule

If the **last letter of the stem** is in **'t kofschip** (*t, k, f, s, ch, p*):

→ add **-te** (singular) / **-ten** (plural)

Otherwise:

→ add **-de** (singular) / **-den** (plural)

| Infinitive | Stem | Last letter | Singular | Plural |
|------------|------|-------------|----------|--------|
| werken | werk | k ✓ | werk**te** | werk**ten** |
| stoppen | stop | p ✓ | stop**te** | stop**ten** |
| leven | leef | f ✓ | leef**te** | leef**ten** |
| wonen | woon | n ✗ | woon**de** | woon**den** |
| bellen | bel | l ✗ | bel**de** | bel**den** |
| reizen | reis | s ✓ | reis**te** | reis**ten** |

> **One form for all persons** — unlike the present tense, the simple past has the same form for every subject. *Ik werkte, jij werkte, hij werkte, wij werkten.*

---

## Full conjugation table

| Subject | -te verb (werken) | -de verb (wonen) |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| ik | werk**te** | woon**de** |
| jij/je | werk**te** | woon**de** |
| u | werk**te** | woon**de** |
| hij/zij/het | werk**te** | woon**de** |
| wij/we | werk**ten** | woon**den** |
| jullie | werk**ten** | woon**den** |
| zij/ze (pl.) | werk**ten** | woon**den** |

> No *t*-drop in the simple past — *jij werkte* stays *werkte* in all positions.

---

## When to use simple past vs perfect

| Situation | Preferred form | Example |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Spoken past (action/event) | **Perfect** | *Ik heb gisteren gewerkt.* |
| Written narrative | **Simple past** | *Hij werkte elke dag hard.* |
| *zijn*, *hebben* in speech | **Simple past** | *Ze was moe. Hij had honger.* |
| Modals in speech | **Simple past** | *Ik kon niet komen.* |
| Habitual past (*vroeger*) | **Simple past** | *Vroeger woonden we in Utrecht.* |

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Vroeger **werkte** hij in Amsterdam. | He used to work in Amsterdam. |
| Ze **woonde** lang in Brussel. | She lived in Brussels for a long time. |
| Het **regende** de hele dag. | It rained all day. |
| We **belden** hem, maar hij nam niet op. | We called him, but he didn't answer. |
| De kinderen **speelden** in de tuin. | The children played in the garden. |
| Hij **leefde** eenvoudig. | He lived simply. |

## Common mistakes

**1. Applying *-de* to *'t kofschip* stems**

*werkde* ✗ → *werk**te*** ✓  
*stopde* ✗ → *stop**te*** ✓

**2. Applying *t*-drop rules (there are none)**

The simple past has no *t*-drop. *Jij werkte* stays *werkte* even in questions: *Werkte jij hier?* ✓

**3. Using simple past for single past events in conversation**

In spoken Dutch, *Ik werkte gisteren* sounds slightly formal. *Ik heb gisteren gewerkt* is more natural. Use simple past freely in writing or for habitual past.

---

## Quick reference

```
Stem ends in 't kofschip' (t k f s ch p):  stem + -te / -ten
All other stems:                             stem + -de / -den

Same form for all singular persons.
Plural adds -n:  werkten / woonden
No t-drop in simple past.
```
