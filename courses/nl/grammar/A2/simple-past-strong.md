# Simple past (strong and irregular verbs)

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 20  
**Depends on:** [Simple past (weak verbs)](simple-past.md) | [zijn and hebben](../A1/zijn-en-hebben.md)

---

## Explanation

**Strong verbs** form the simple past by **changing their stem vowel** rather than adding a suffix. There is no *-te* or *-de* — the vowel change carries the past-tense meaning. These must be memorised, but many follow recognisable patterns.

The singular and plural forms differ only in the final *-en*:

```
singular:  stem with changed vowel  (no ending)
plural:    stem with changed vowel + -en
```

## Vowel change patterns

While the changes must be memorised, these groupings can help:

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|---------|
| *ij* → *ee* | rijden→**reed**, blijven→**bleef**, schrijven→**schreef** |
| *ie* → *oo* | vliegen→**vloog**, bieden→**bood** |
| *i* → *o* | drinken→**dronk**, zingen→**zong**, beginnen→**begon** |
| *e* → *a* | eten→**at**, lezen→**las**, geven→**gaf** |
| *a/aa* → *ie* | slapen→**sliep**, laten→**liet**, vallen→**viel** |
| *ij/ui* → *ee/oo* | bijten→**beet**, buigen→**boog** |

## Modals in the simple past

Modal verbs (all weak but irregular) are almost always used in the simple past rather than the perfect in speech:

| Infinitive | Simple past | English |
|------------|------------|---------|
| kunnen | **kon** (pl. **konden**) | could |
| willen | **wilde** / **wou** (pl. **wilden**) | wanted to |
| moeten | **moest** (pl. **moesten**) | had to |
| mogen | **mocht** (pl. **mochten**) | was allowed to |
| zullen | **zou** (pl. **zouden**) | would |

## Full conjugation pattern (strong)

| Subject | rijden (strong) | zijn (irregular) |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| ik | **reed** | **was** |
| jij/je | **reed** | **was** |
| u | **reed** | **was** |
| hij/zij/het | **reed** | **was** |
| wij/we | **reden** | **waren** |
| jullie | **reden** | **waren** |
| zij/ze (pl.) | **reden** | **waren** |

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Hij **was** gisteren ziek. | He was ill yesterday. |
| We **hadden** geen tijd. | We had no time. |
| Ze **ging** naar de markt. | She went to the market. |
| De kinderen **aten** soep. | The children ate soup. |
| Ik **kon** niet slapen. | I could not sleep. |
| Hij **schreef** een lange brief. | He wrote a long letter. |
| We **zagen** een mooie film. | We saw a beautiful film. |
| Ze **bleven** thuis omdat het regende. | They stayed home because it was raining. |

## Common mistakes

**1. Adding *-te/-de* to strong verbs**

*rijdde* ✗ → *reed* ✓  
*zegde* ✗ → *zei* ✓

**2. Using *was* for plural**

*Wij was thuis.* ✗ → *Wij **waren** thuis.* ✓

**3. Using perfect of modals in speech**

*Ik heb niet kunnen slapen.* — grammatically correct but less natural in a simple past context.  
*Ik **kon** niet slapen.* ✓ — the preferred spoken form.

---

## Quick reference

```
Strong verbs: vowel change in stem, no suffix
  singular:  changed stem only   (ik reed, hij reed)
  plural:    changed stem + -en  (wij reden)

Key irregulars to learn first:
  zijn → was/waren
  hebben → had/hadden
  gaan → ging/gingen
  komen → kwam/kwamen

Modals use simple past in speech:
  kon / wilde (wou) / moest / mocht / zou
```
