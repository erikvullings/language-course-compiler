# Ordinal numbers

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 27  
**Depends on:** [Numbers and counting](../A1/cardinal-numbers.md)

---

## Explanation

Ordinal numbers (*rangtelwoorden*) express rank or order: first, second, third. Dutch forms them by adding **-de** or **-ste** to the cardinal number.

---

## The two suffixes

| Suffix | Used for | Examples |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **-de** | most numbers (2nd–19th, some tens) | tweede, derde, vierde |
| **-ste** | 1st, 8th, and numbers ending in *-tig* (20th, 30th …) | eerste, achtste, twintigste |

---

## Tens and compounds

Numbers ending in *-tig* take **-ste**:

| Cardinal | Ordinal |
|----------|---------|
| twintig | **twintigste** |
| dertig | **dertigste** |
| veertig | **veertigste** |
| vijftig | **vijftigste** |
| honderd | **honderdste** |
| duizend | **duizendste** |

Compound numbers: the ordinal suffix goes on the last element only:

| Cardinal | Ordinal |
|----------|---------|
| eenentwintig | **eenentwintigste** |
| tweeëndertig | **tweeëndertigste** |
| vijfenveertig | **vijfenveertigste** |

---

## Using ordinals: adjective endings apply

Ordinals are adjectives and follow the same *-e* ending rules as regular adjectives:

| Position | Example | English |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Attributive (de-word) | de **eerste** dag | the first day |
| Attributive (het-word) | het **tweede** huis | the second house |
| Attributive (*een* + het-word) | een **derde** boek | a third book |
| Predicative | Hij is de **eerste**. | He is the first. |

---

## Dates

Dutch dates follow the pattern: **ordinal + month (+ year)**:

> *de eerste januari* — the first of January  
> *de vijfde maart tweeduizend vijfentwintig* — 5 March 2025

In writing, dates are often abbreviated with a period after the numeral:

> *1 januari* / *1 jan.* / *1-1* (day-month)

When saying a date in a sentence:

> *Mijn verjaardag is op **de tiende februari**.* — My birthday is on the tenth of February.

---

## Other uses

| Use | Dutch | English |
|-----|-------|---------|
| Ranking | Hij eindigde als **tweede**. | He finished second. |
| Floor / storey | Ik woon op de **derde** verdieping. | I live on the third floor. |
| Sequence | Voor de **eerste** keer. | For the first time. |
| Fractions (with *de* + ordinal) | **een derde** (1/3) | one third |

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Het is vandaag de **zevende** april. | Today is the seventh of April. |
| Ze won de **eerste** prijs. | She won first prize. |
| Dit is mijn **derde** kopje koffie. | This is my third cup of coffee. |
| Hij woont op de **tweede** verdieping. | He lives on the second floor. |
| Voor de **twintigste** keer — nee! | For the twentieth time — no! |
| Een **kwart** is de **vierde** deel van iets. | A quarter is the fourth part of something. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *-de* for *eerste* and *achtste***

*eende* ✗ → ***eerste*** ✓  
*achtde* ✗ → ***achtste*** ✓

**2. Using *-de* for *-tig* numbers**

*twintigde* ✗ → ***twintigste*** ✓

**3. Forgetting the adjective *-e* ending**

*de eerste dag* ✓ but *een eerste boek* → *een **eerste** boek* ✓ (het-word with *een* — no *-e*... wait, *boek* is a het-word: *een eerste boek* — ordinal before het-word with *een* → no extra *-e* needed since the *-e* is part of the ordinal suffix itself)

> The ordinal's *-e* or *-ste* already serves as the adjective ending — don't add another *-e* on top.

---

## Quick reference

```
-de:   2e (tweede), 3e (derde), 4e–7e, 9e–19e
-ste:  1e (eerste), 8e (achtste), -tig numbers (20e, 30e …), 100e, 1000e

Dates:  de [ordinal] [month]  →  de vijfde maart
Fractions:  een [ordinal]  →  een derde (⅓), een vierde (¼)
```
