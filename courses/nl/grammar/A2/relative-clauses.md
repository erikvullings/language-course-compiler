# Relative clauses (die / dat)

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 24  
**Depends on:** [Subordinate clauses](subordinate-clauses.md) | [Definite articles](../A1/articles.md)

---

## Explanation

A **relative clause** (*betrekkelijke bijzin*) gives extra information about a noun. It is introduced by a **relative pronoun** that refers back to that noun (the *antecedent*). Dutch uses two relative pronouns:

| Relative pronoun | Used for |
|-----------------|---------|
| **die** | *de*-words (singular) and all plurals |
| **dat** | *het*-words (singular only) |

The pattern exactly mirrors the demonstratives *deze/die* and *dit/dat*:

```
de fiets  →  de fiets die ik heb gekocht
het huis  →  het huis dat we zoeken
```

The verb in the relative clause goes to the **end** (same rule as all subordinate clauses).

---

## Choosing *die* or *dat*

| Antecedent | Relative pronoun | Example |
|------------|-----------------|---------|
| *de*-word, singular | **die** | de man **die** daar staat |
| *het*-word, singular | **dat** | het boek **dat** ik lees |
| any plural | **die** | de kinderen **die** spelen |

> **Memory hook:** same rule as articles and demonstratives — *het*-word singular → *dat*; everything else → *die*.

---

## Word order inside the relative clause

The finite verb goes to the end, just as in any subordinate clause:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| de vrouw die **werkt** | the woman who works |
| de auto die hij **heeft gekocht** | the car that he bought |
| het huis dat we **zoeken** | the house that we are looking for |
| de kinderen die buiten **spelen** | the children who are playing outside |

---

## The relative pronoun as subject or object

The relative pronoun can be the **subject** or the **object** of the relative clause. The form (*die/dat*) does not change:

| Function | Dutch | English |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Subject | De man **die** hier **woont** is aardig. | The man who lives here is nice. |
| Object | De man **die** ik **ken** is aardig. | The man whom I know is nice. |
| Object | Het boek **dat** ik **lees** is interessant. | The book that I am reading is interesting. |

---

## Relative clause with a preposition

When the relative clause contains a preposition referring to a **person**, use *preposition + wie*:

> *De vriend **met wie** ik werk.* — The friend with whom I work.  
> *De leraar **voor wie** ze studeert.* — The teacher for whom she is studying.

When the preposition refers to a **thing**, use *waar + preposition* (a pronominal adverb):

> *Het boek **waar**over ik praat.* — The book I am talking about.  
> *De stoel **waar**op hij zit.* — The chair on which he is sitting.  
> *Het huis **waar**in ze woont.* — The house in which she lives.

---

## *wat* as relative pronoun

After indefinite antecedents (*alles, iets, niets, veel, weinig*) and after a whole clause, use **wat**:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Alles** wat hij zegt is waar. | Everything he says is true. |
| **Iets** wat ik niet begrijp. | Something I don't understand. |
| Hij slaagde, **wat** iedereen verraste. | He passed, which surprised everyone. |

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| De fiets **die** ik gisteren heb gekocht, is al kapot. | The bicycle I bought yesterday is already broken. |
| Hoe heet het restaurant **dat** jullie aanraden? | What is the restaurant called that you recommend? |
| De mensen **die** hier wonen zijn erg aardig. | The people who live here are very friendly. |
| Dit is het huis **dat** ik wil kopen. | This is the house I want to buy. |
| De film **waarover** ze praten, ken ik niet. | The film they are talking about, I don't know. |
| Er is **niets wat** ik kan doen. | There is nothing I can do. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *dat* for a *de*-word**

*de man dat daar staat* ✗ → *de man **die** daar staat* ✓

**2. Using *die* for a singular *het*-word**

*het huis die we zoeken* ✗ → *het huis **dat** we zoeken* ✓

**3. Keeping the verb in position 2**

*de man die **hij is** aardig* ✗ → *de man die aardig **is*** ✓

**4. Using *dat* instead of *wat* after indefinite antecedents**

*alles dat hij zegt* ✗ → *alles **wat** hij zegt* ✓

---

## Quick reference

```
die  →  de-words (sg.) + all plurals
dat  →  het-words (singular only)
wat  →  after alles, iets, niets, or a whole clause

Verb goes to the end of the relative clause.

With preposition:
  people:  preposition + wie   (met wie, voor wie)
  things:  waar + preposition  (waarover, waarop, waarmee)
```
