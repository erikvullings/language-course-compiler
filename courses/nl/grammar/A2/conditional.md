# Conditional (zou / zouden)

**CEFR level:** A2  
**Introduced in lesson:** 29  
**Depends on:** [Future with zullen](future-with-zullen.md)

---

## Explanation

**Zou / zouden** is the simple past form of *zullen*, and it functions as the Dutch conditional — equivalent to English *would*. It is used for:

- **polite requests**: *Zou jij dat kunnen doen?*
- **hypothetical situations**: *Als ik rijk was, zou ik reizen.*
- **reported / indirect speech**: *Ze zei dat ze zou komen.*

---

## Conjugation

| Subject | Form |
|---------|------|
| ik | **zou** |
| jij/je | **zou** |
| u | **zou** |
| hij/zij/het | **zou** |
| wij/we | **zouden** |
| jullie | **zouden** |
| zij/ze (pl.) | **zouden** |

> One form for all singular persons — no *t*-drop, no irregularities.

---

## 1. Polite requests and suggestions

*Zou/zouden* softens a request, similar to English *would* or *could*:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Zou** jij dit kunnen doen? | Would you be able to do this? |
| **Zou** u mij kunnen helpen? | Could you help me? *(formal)* |
| **Zou** jij de deur willen sluiten? | Would you close the door? |
| Ik **zou** graag een tafel reserveren. | I would like to reserve a table. |
| **Zouden** jullie morgen kunnen komen? | Would you (all) be able to come tomorrow? |

> *Zou … graag* is one of the most useful polite formulas: *Ik zou graag …* — I would like to …

---

## 2. Hypothetical situations (*als* … *zou*)

The conditional is used in *als*-sentences (if-clauses) to express hypotheticals. The *als*-clause uses the simple past (or *zou* for the consequence):

```
Als  [simple past / zou clause]  ,  [zou + infinitive]
Als  ik meer tijd had             ,  zou ik meer lezen.
If   I had more time              ,  I would read more.
```

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Als ik jou was, **zou** ik het vragen. | If I were you, I would ask. |
| Als het niet regende, **zouden** we fietsen. | If it weren't raining, we would cycle. |
| **Zou** jij komen als ik je uitnodigde? | Would you come if I invited you? |

> Note: Dutch uses the simple past (*had, was, regende*) in the *als*-clause, not a subjunctive form. The consequence clause uses *zou(den) + infinitive*.

---

## 3. Reported / indirect speech

When reporting what someone said about the future, *zal/zullen* shifts to *zou/zouden*:

| Direct speech | Reported speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| *"Ik **zal** komen."* | Ze zei dat ze **zou** komen. |
| *"We **zullen** helpen."* | Hij beloofde dat ze **zouden** helpen. |
| *"Het **zal** niet lang duren."* | Ze dacht dat het niet lang **zou** duren. |

---

## *zou* without *als* — softened assertion

*Zou* is also used on its own to soften a statement, express uncertainty or hedge:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Dat **zou** kunnen. | That could be / that might be the case. |
| Hij **zou** ziek zijn. | He is said to be ill. / Apparently he is ill. |
| Ze **zou** om negen uur komen. | She was supposed to come at nine. |

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Zou** jij me een plezier willen doen? | Would you do me a favour? |
| Ik **zou** graag meer reizen. | I would like to travel more. |
| Als ik kon, **zou** ik elke dag zwemmen. | If I could, I would swim every day. |
| Ze zei dat ze later **zou** komen. | She said that she would come later. |
| **Zouden** we morgen kunnen afspreken? | Could we meet tomorrow? |
| Dat **zou** een goed idee zijn. | That would be a good idea. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *zal* instead of *zou* for hypotheticals**

*Als ik rijk was, zal ik reizen.* ✗ → *Als ik rijk was, **zou** ik reizen.* ✓

**2. Using the present tense in both clauses of a hypothetical**

*Als ik rijk ben, reis ik.* — this is possible but means *if/when I am (actually) rich* (real condition).  
Use simple past in the *als*-clause for an unreal/hypothetical condition: *Als ik rijk **was***…

**3. Conjugating the infinitive after *zou***

*Hij zou gaat.* ✗ → *Hij zou **gaan**.* ✓

---

## Quick reference

```
zou (sg.) / zouden (pl.)  =  conditional (would)

Polite requests:     Zou jij … kunnen …? / Ik zou graag …
Hypothetical:        Als [simple past], zou(den) [infinitive]
Reported speech:     zal → zou / zullen → zouden (after past tense verb)
Softened assertion:  Dat zou kunnen. / Hij zou ziek zijn.
```
