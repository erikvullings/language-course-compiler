# Plural nouns

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 7  
**Depends on:** [Definite articles](articles.md)

---

## Explanation

Dutch forms plurals by adding **-en** or **-s** to the singular. There is no single rule that covers every word, but the patterns below handle the vast majority of everyday vocabulary.

> **Key fact:** all plural nouns take the article **de**, regardless of the singular article.

---

## The two plural endings

### -en (most common)

Add *-en* to the singular. Spelling may change to preserve the original vowel sound.

| Singular | Plural | Note |
|----------|--------|------|
| de fiets | de fietsen | straightforward |
| het huis | de huizen | *s* → *z* between vowels (voicing) |
| de dag | de dagen | open syllable: single *g* + *en* |
| het boek | de boeken | straightforward |
| de man | de mannen | short vowel + single consonant → double consonant |
| de boom | de bomen | long vowel: double *oo* → single *o* (open syllable) |

**Spelling rules for -en plurals:**

1. **Short vowel + single consonant** → double the consonant: *man* → *mannen*, *kat* → *katten*
2. **Long vowel written double** → write single before *-en* (open syllable): *boom* → *bomen*, *tafel* (already ends in *-el*, no change)
3. **-s between vowels** → becomes *-z-*: *huis* → *huizen*, *roos* → *rozen*
4. **-f between vowels** → becomes *-v-*: *brief* → *brieven*, *dief* → *dieven*

### -s (common with certain endings)

Words ending in an unstressed syllable — especially *-el*, *-em*, *-en*, *-er*, *-ie*, *-aar*, *-eur* — usually take *-s*:

| Singular | Plural |
|----------|--------|
| de lepel | de lepels |
| de tafel | de tafels |
| de broer | de broers |
| de auto | de auto's *(apostrophe before -s after a bare vowel)* |
| het café | de café's |
| de foto | de foto's |

> After a bare vowel (*a, e, i, o, u* at the end), an apostrophe is inserted before the *-s* to prevent mispronunciation: *auto's*, not *autos*.

---

## Irregular plurals (must be memorised)

A small set of very common nouns have irregular plurals:

| Singular | Plural | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| het kind | de kinderen | child / children |
| het ei | de eieren | egg / eggs |
| de stad | de steden | city / cities |

---

## All plurals take *de*

| Singular | Article | Plural | Article |
|----------|---------|--------|---------|
| de fiets | de | de fietsen | de |
| **het** huis | **het** | de huizen | **de** |
| **het** boek | **het** | de boeken | **de** |

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik heb twee **katten**. | I have two cats. |
| De **bomen** zijn groen. | The trees are green. |
| Ze leest veel **boeken**. | She reads many books. |
| De **kinderen** spelen buiten. | The children play outside. |
| Hij heeft drie **auto's**. | He has three cars. |
| De **tafels** zijn schoon. | The tables are clean. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Doubling the consonant when it is not needed**

Only short vowels before a single consonant need doubling: *dag* → *dagen* (not *daggen*), because *dag* already has one consonant.

**2. Not doubling when it is needed**

*man* → *manen* ✗ (that would be *moons*!) → *mannen* ✓

**3. Using *het* with plural nouns**

*het boeken* ✗ → *de boeken* ✓

**4. Omitting the apostrophe before -s**

*autos* ✗ → *auto's* ✓

---

## Quick reference

```
-en   most nouns (apply spelling adjustments)
-s    unstressed final syllable (-el, -er, -ie, -aar, -eur, bare vowel)
-eren  a handful of irregular nouns (kind → kinderen)

All plurals → de (never het)
```
