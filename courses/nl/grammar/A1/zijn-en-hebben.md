# zijn and hebben

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 3  
**Depends on:** [Present tense](present-tense.md)

---

## Explanation

**Zijn** (to be) and **hebben** (to have) are the two most frequently used verbs in Dutch. Both are irregular — their forms do not follow the regular stem + ending pattern, so they must be memorised.

They are also the two **auxiliary verbs** used to build the perfect tense (covered later in [Perfect tense](perfect-tense.md)), so getting comfortable with them early pays off many times over.

---

## zijn — to be

| Subject | Form |
|---------|------|
| ik | **ben** |
| jij/je | **bent** |
| u | **bent** / **is** |
| hij/zij/het | **is** |
| wij/we | **zijn** |
| jullie | **zijn** |
| zij/ze (pl.) | **zijn** |

> *u bent* is the standard polite form; *u is* occurs in writing and formal speech.

**Inversion with jij/je:**  
*Ben* **jij** student? — Are you a student? (no *t*-drop because *ben* already has no *-t*)

---

## hebben — to have

| Subject | Form |
|---------|------|
| ik | **heb** |
| jij/je | **hebt** |
| u | **heeft** |
| hij/zij/het | **heeft** |
| wij/we | **hebben** |
| jullie | **hebben** |
| zij/ze (pl.) | **hebben** |

**Inversion with jij/je:**  
*Heb* **jij** een auto? → *-t* drops: *heb* (not *hebt*)

---

## Uses of **zijn**

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Identity / profession | Ik **ben** leraar. | I am a teacher. |
| Nationality | Hij **is** Nederlands. | He is Dutch. |
| Location | Ze **zijn** in Amsterdam. | They are in Amsterdam. |
| State / condition | Het **is** koud. | It is cold. |
| Telling the time | Het **is** drie uur. | It is three o'clock. |

---

## Uses of **hebben**

| Use | Example | English |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Possession | Ik **heb** een fiets. | I have a bicycle. |
| Hunger / thirst / pain | Hij **heeft** dorst. | He is thirsty. |
| Age | Ze **heeft** twintig jaar. | She is twenty years old. |

> Note: Dutch says *ik heb honger* (I have hunger) where English says *I am hungry*.

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik **ben** moe. | I am tired. |
| **Ben** jij student? | Are you a student? |
| Hij **is** mijn broer. | He is my brother. |
| Wij **zijn** thuis. | We are at home. |
| Ik **heb** een hond. | I have a dog. |
| **Heb** jij tijd? | Do you have time? |
| Ze **hebben** honger. | They are hungry. |
| U **heeft** een vraag. | You have a question. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *hebben* where *zijn* is needed**

*Ik heb moe.* ✗ → *Ik **ben** moe.* ✓  
*Ik heb twintig jaar.* is correct — age uses *hebben* in Dutch.

**2. Confusing *zijn* (to be) and *zijn* (his)**

*zijn* can be a verb or a possessive pronoun. Word order makes it clear:  
- *Dat **is** zijn fiets.* — That is his bicycle. (verb | pronoun)

**3. *t*-drop with *hebben***

*Heb**t** jij een auto?* ✗ → ***Heb** jij een auto?* ✓  
The *t* always drops when *jij/je* follows the verb.

---

## Quick reference

```
zijn:    ben / bent / is / zijn
hebben:  heb / hebt / heeft / hebben
```
