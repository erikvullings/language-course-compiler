# Imperative

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 16  
**Depends on:** [Present tense](present-tense.md)

---

## Explanation

The imperative (*gebiedende wijs*) is used to give commands, instructions, requests, and invitations. Dutch imperatives are formed simply: **use the stem of the verb — nothing added**.

> Stem = infinitive minus *-en*  
> *werken* → stem **werk** → *Werk!* (Work!)

---

## Forming the imperative

| Infinitive | Stem | Imperative | English |
|------------|------|-----------|---------|
| komen | kom | **Kom!** | Come! |
| gaan | ga | **Ga!** | Go! |
| zitten | zit | **Zit!** | Sit! |
| staan | sta | **Sta op!** | Stand up! |
| luisteren | luister | **Luister!** | Listen! |
| wachten | wacht | **Wacht!** | Wait! |
| kijken | kijk | **Kijk!** | Look! |
| schrijven | schrijf | **Schrijf!** | Write! |
| lezen | lees | **Lees!** | Read! |
| eten | eet | **Eet!** | Eat! |

No subject pronoun is used with a standard imperative — the verb stands alone.

---

## Adding politeness

The bare imperative can sound brusque. Dutch softens commands in several ways:

| Strategy | Dutch | English |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Add *alsjeblieft* (informal) or *alstublieft* (formal) | *Kom **alsjeblieft** hier.* | Come here, please. |
| Add *eens* (softer request) | *Kijk **eens** hier.* | Just have a look here. |
| Add *even* (brief / casual) | *Wacht **even**.* | Just wait a moment. |
| Add *maar* (encouraging) | *Ga **maar** zitten.* | Go ahead and sit down. |

---

## Imperative with *u* (formal)

When addressing someone formally, add **u** after the verb:

| Informal | Formal |
|---------|--------|
| *Kom hier!* | *Komt **u** hier!* |
| *Ga zitten!* | *Gaat **u** zitten!* |

> The formal imperative uses the stem + *t* (third person singular form) + *u*.

---

## Imperative with *jullie* (plural)

For a group, use the infinitive form — it sounds like the plural present:

| Singular | Plural (group) |
|---------|---------------|
| *Luister!* | *Luister**en** jullie!* / *Luister!* |

In practice, the singular imperative is widely used for groups too, especially in instructions and signage.

---

## Common uses

| Context | Dutch | English |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Commands | *Kom hier!* | Come here! |
| Instructions | *Ga rechtdoor, dan links.* | Go straight, then left. |
| Recipes | *Voeg zout toe.* | Add salt. |
| Invitations | *Ga zitten, alsjeblieft.* | Please sit down. |
| Encouragement | *Doe maar rustig aan.* | Just take it easy. |
| Signs | *Niet roken.* | No smoking. *(infinitive used for public notices)* |

> Public notices and signs typically use the **infinitive** (not the stem): *Niet roken*, *Hier betalen*. This is a written convention, not a grammar rule for conversation.

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Luister** goed naar de leraar. | Listen carefully to the teacher. |
| **Schrijf** je naam hier. | Write your name here. |
| **Eet** je bord leeg. | Finish your plate. |
| **Wacht** even — ik kom eraan. | Just wait — I'm coming. |
| **Kijk** eens hoe mooi het is! | Just look how beautiful it is! |
| **Ga** naar bed, het is laat. | Go to bed, it is late. |

## Common mistakes

**1. Adding a subject pronoun**

*Jij kom hier!* ✗ → *Kom hier!* ✓  
*(Adding a pronoun is possible for strong emphasis, but unusual)*

**2. Using infinitive instead of stem in conversation**

*Komen hier!* ✗ → *Kom hier!* ✓  
*(Infinitive is for signs, not spoken commands)*

**3. Adding -t to the stem**

*Werkt!* ✗ → *Werk!* ✓  
The imperative is always the bare stem, no ending.

---

## Quick reference

```
Imperative  =  verb stem  (no pronoun, no -t, no -en)
Polite:     add alsjeblieft / eens / even / maar
Formal:     stem + t + u  (Komt u hier!)
```
