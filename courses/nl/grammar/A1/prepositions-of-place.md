# Prepositions of place

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 13  
**Depends on:** [Definite articles](articles.md)

---

## Explanation

Prepositions of place (*plaatsbepalingen*) describe where something is located. Dutch prepositions are invariable — they do not change form. However, the noun phrase that follows a preposition uses a specific article form called the **dative** in older grammar, though at A1 level you only need to know that *de* stays *de* and *het* stays *het* after most simple prepositions.

---

## Core prepositions of place

| Dutch | English | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **in** | in / inside | *De sleutels zijn **in** de tas.* |
| **op** | on / on top of / at (surface) | *Het boek ligt **op** de tafel.* |
| **onder** | under / below | *De kat zit **onder** de stoel.* |
| **naast** | next to / beside | *De bank staat **naast** het raam.* |
| **achter** | behind | *Hij staat **achter** de deur.* |
| **voor** | in front of / before | *De auto staat **voor** het huis.* |
| **tussen** | between | *De bank staat **tussen** de twee stoelen.* |
| **boven** | above / over | *De lamp hangt **boven** de tafel.* |
| **bij** | near / at (someone's place) | *Ze woont **bij** de universiteit.* |
| **tegenover** | opposite / across from | *Het cafe zit **tegenover** het station.* |

---

## **in** vs **op** — the key contrast

Dutch distinguishes containment (*in*) from surface contact (*op*) more strictly than English:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| De melk zit **in** de koelkast. | The milk is in the fridge. |
| Het glas staat **op** de tafel. | The glass is on the table. |
| Hij werkt **op** kantoor. | He works at the office. *(surface/location)* |
| Ze wonen **in** Amsterdam. | They live in Amsterdam. *(enclosed place)* |

---

## Common location phrases

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **thuis** | at home |
| **op school** | at school |
| **op het werk** | at work |
| **op de markt** | at the market |
| **in de winkel** | in the shop |
| **in het park** | in the park |
| **op straat** | on the street |
| **aan de rechterkant** | on the right side |
| **aan de linkerkant** | on the left side |

> *Thuis* is an adverb, not a prepositional phrase; it stands alone without a preposition.

---

## Verbs used with location

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **staan** | to stand (vertical objects: cups, books, people) |
| **liggen** | to lie (horizontal objects: papers, people resting) |
| **zitten** | to sit (people, animals, items in containers) |
| **hangen** | to hang |

Choosing the right positional verb is a feature of native Dutch. At A1, focus on getting the preposition right; the positional verbs will become natural over time.

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| De kat **zit** **op** de stoel. | The cat is sitting on the chair. |
| Mijn boek **ligt** **onder** het bed. | My book is under the bed. |
| Het station is **naast** het park. | The station is next to the park. |
| Ze wachten **voor** de deur. | They are waiting in front of the door. |
| De supermarkt zit **tussen** de bank en de apotheek. | The supermarket is between the bank and the pharmacy. |
| Hij woont **in** een groot huis. | He lives in a big house. |
| De lamp **hangt** **boven** de tafel. | The lamp hangs above the table. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Translating English *at* as *op* in all cases**

*at home* → *thuis* (not *op huis*)  
*at school* → *op school* ✓  
*at the station* → *op het station* / *bij het station*

**2. Using *in* for surface locations**

*Het boek ligt **in** de tafel.* ✗ → *op de tafel* ✓

**3. Forgetting that *tussen* takes two objects**

*De stoel staat tussen de tafel.* ✗ → *De stoel staat **tussen** de tafel **en de muur**.* ✓

---

## Quick reference

```
in      →  inside an enclosed space or area
op      →  on a surface or at a functional location
onder   →  below / underneath
naast   →  next to
achter  →  behind
voor    →  in front of
tussen  →  between (requires two reference points)
boven   →  above
bij     →  near / at (informal location)
tegenover → opposite
```
