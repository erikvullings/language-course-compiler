# Definite articles (de / het)

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 4  
**Depends on:** —

---

## Explanation

Dutch has **two definite articles**, both equivalent to English *the*:

| Article | Used with           | Example                                                               |
| ------- | ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **de**  | common-gender nouns | *de man* (the man), *de vrouw* (the woman), *de fiets* (the bicycle)  |
| **het** | neuter-gender nouns | *het huis* (the house), *het kind* (the child), *het boek* (the book) |

About **two-thirds** of Dutch nouns are *de*-words; about **one-third** are *het*-words.

> **Critical advice:** always learn the article together with the noun. Do not try to derive the article from the meaning or the spelling — for most words there is no reliable rule. Memorise *het huis*, not just *huis*.

---

## Why it matters

The article affects:
- adjective endings: *de groot**e** fiets* vs *het groot**e** huis* (but: *een groot huis*, no *-e*)
- demonstratives: *de**ze** fiets* vs *di**t** huis*
- pronoun replacement: a *de*-word becomes *hij/hem*; a *het*-word becomes *het*

Getting the article wrong doesn't usually block communication, but it is one of the most visible markers of fluency.

---

## Patterns that help (but have exceptions)

These patterns are tendencies, not rules. Use them as a memory aid, not a guarantee.

| Pattern                                            | Tends to be  | Examples                    |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ------------ | --------------------------- |
| People and animals (natural gender)                | *de*         | *de man, de vrouw, de hond* |
| Nouns ending in *-ing*                             | *de*         | *de vergadering, de woning* |
| Nouns ending in *-heid*, *-heid*, *-nis*, *-schap* | *de*         | *de vrijheid, de kennis*    |
| Nouns ending in *-ment*, *-sel*, *-aal*            | often *het*  | *het cement, het lichaam*   |
| Diminutives (words ending in *-je*)                | always *het* | *het meisje, het huisje*    |
| Infinitives used as nouns                          | always *het* | *het lopen* (the running)   |

---

## Plural nouns always take *de*

Regardless of the singular article, **all plural nouns** use *de*:

| Singular   | Plural        |
| ---------- | ------------- |
| *de fiets* | *de fietsen*  |
| *het huis* | *de huizen*   |
| *het kind* | *de kinderen* |

---

## Examples

| Dutch                             | English                    |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| **De** fiets staat buiten.        | The bicycle is outside.    |
| **Het** huis is groot.            | The house is big.          |
| **De** kinderen spelen.           | The children play.         |
| **De** leraar spreekt Nederlands. | The teacher speaks Dutch.  |
| **Het** boek is interessant.      | The book is interesting.   |
| **De** honden lopen in het park.  | The dogs walk in the park. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Guessing *de* for everything**

Because *de* is more common, beginners default to it. But many everyday words are *het*-words: *het huis, het kind, het boek, het water, het brood*.

**2. Forgetting that plurals always take *de***

*het kinderen* ✗ → *de kinderen* ✓

**3. Not learning the article with the noun**

Trying to retroactively assign articles once you know many words leads to persistent errors. From day one, store *de fiets*, *het huis* — not *fiets*, *huis*.

---

## Quick reference

```text
de  →  common gender (~2/3 of nouns) + all plurals
het →  neuter gender (~1/3 of nouns)
      always: diminutives (-je), nominalised infinitives
```
