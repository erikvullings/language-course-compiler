## Explanation

Dutch cardinal numbers (counting numbers) are needed from the very first conversations: prices, ages, addresses, times, quantities. The system is regular beyond 12, with one structural quirk: from 13 upward, numbers are spoken **units-before-tens** (*three-and-twenty* rather than *twenty-three*).

## Compound numbers (21–99): units *en* tens

Dutch puts the **units first**, joined with **en** (and):

| Number | Dutch | Literally |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| 21 | **eenentwintig** | one-and-twenty |
| 32 | **tweeëndertig** | two-and-thirty |
| 45 | **vijfenveertig** | five-and-forty |
| 58 | **achtenvijftig** | eight-and-fifty |
| 99 | **negenennegentig** | nine-and-ninety |

> The *-en-* connector is written as one word. When the units end in a vowel and the tens begin with a vowel, a diaeresis (¨) or hyphen appears: *tweeëndertig*.

## Hundreds and thousands

| Number | Dutch |
|--------|-------|
| 100 | **honderd** |
| 200 | **tweehonderd** |
| 1 000 | **duizend** |
| 1 000 000 | **een miljoen** |

Compound: *driehonderd vijfenvijftig* (355)

> Dutch uses a **period** (not a comma) to separate thousands: *1.000* = one thousand. Decimals use a **comma**: *3,14*.

## Using numbers with nouns

Numbers are used directly before nouns — no article, no extra word:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **twee** boeken | two books |
| **vijf** euro | five euros |
| **tien** minuten | ten minutes |

The noun stays in the **singular** after *één* but is **plural** after all other numbers:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| één fiets | one bicycle |
| twee fietsen | two bicycles |

## Common mistakes

**1. Units-before-tens order**

*twintigeen* ✗ → *eenentwintig* ✓

**2. Forgetting *één* for the numeral**

*een* (without accent) is the indefinite article; *één* (with accent) is the number 1. In speech they sound different; in writing use the accent for the numeral.

**3. Plural after all numbers except 1**

*drie boek* ✗ → *drie boeken* ✓

## Quick reference

```
1–12: must be memorised (elf, twaalf are irregular)
13–19: [root]+tien
20–90: twintig / dertig / veertig / vijftig / zestig / zeventig / tachtig / negentig
21–99: [units] + en + [tens]  →  written as one word
```
