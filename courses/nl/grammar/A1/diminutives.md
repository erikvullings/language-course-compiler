# Diminutives (-je)

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 15  
**Depends on:** [Definite articles](articles.md)

---

## Explanation

The diminutive (*het verkleinwoord*) is one of the most productive and culturally important features of Dutch. Nearly every noun can form a diminutive by adding a **-je** suffix (with variants). Diminutives express:

- small size: *een huisje* (a small house / a cottage)
- affection or endearment: *een kopje koffie* (a nice cup of coffee)
- informality and coziness: *een feestje* (a little party)
- abstract smallness or a bit of something: *een beetje* (a little bit)

> **All diminutives are het-words**, regardless of the gender of the base noun.

---

## The five suffix variants

The correct suffix depends on the **final sound** of the base noun.

| Suffix | Condition | Examples |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **-je** | ends in a long vowel, *l*, *r*, *n*, *-ng*, *-nk* | *boom → boom**tje***, *raam → raam**pje*** |

Wait — let me lay this out more carefully:

| Suffix | Used after | Examples |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **-je** | long vowel or diphthong | *tafel → tafel**tje*** *(exception: schwa-final)* |
| **-tje** | *l, r, n, -ng, -nk*, or short vowel + *w* | *boom → boom**tje***, *raam → raam**tje***, *ding → din**getje*** |
| **-etje** | short vowel + *l, r, n, m* | *bal → bal**letje***, *dam → dam**metje*** |
| **-pje** | ends in *-m* preceded by a short vowel | *boom* does not apply; *arm → arm**pje*** |
| **-kje** | ends in *-ng* | *ring → rin**getje*** |

> At A1 level, it is more practical to **memorise the most common forms** than to apply all phonological rules. Focus on the forms you encounter in lessons.

## Always a *het*-word

| Base noun | Article | Diminutive | Article |
|-----------|---------|------------|---------|
| *de fiets* | de | *fietsje* | **het** |
| *de man* | de | *mannetje* | **het** |
| *het huis* | het | *huisje* | **het** |
| *de tafel* | de | *tafeltje* | **het** |

## Common everyday diminutives

Many diminutives have become so common that they are the default word in speech:

| Diminutive | English |
|------------|---------|
| **het kopje** | cup (coffee cup) |
| **het meisje** | girl |
| **het jongetje** | little boy |
| **het huisje** | cottage / small house |
| **het beetje** | a bit / a little |
| **het kaartje** | ticket / card |
| **het broodje** | bread roll |
| **het autootje** | little car / toy car |
| **het woordje** | (a) word |
| **het feestje** | party |

> *een beetje* (a little bit) is one of the most frequently used expressions in Dutch and is always a diminutive.

---

## Cultural note

Dutch speakers use diminutives far more than other languages do. A *kopje koffie* (lit. *a little cup of coffee*) is just a normal cup of coffee — the diminutive signals warmth and conviviality, not tiny size. Avoiding all diminutives sounds stilted and formal; using them naturally is a sign of Dutch fluency.

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Wil je een **kopje** koffie? | Would you like a cup of coffee? |
| Ze heeft een **klein huisje** in Zeeland. | She has a small cottage in Zeeland. |
| Het **meisje** speelt in de tuin. | The girl plays in the garden. |
| Mag ik een **kaartje**, alstublieft? | May I have a ticket, please? |
| Ik wil maar een **beetje** suiker. | I just want a little bit of sugar. |
| Hij eet altijd een **broodje** 's middags. | He always eats a bread roll in the afternoon. |

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *de* with a diminutive**

*de huisje* ✗ → ***het** huisje* ✓ (all diminutives are *het*-words)

**2. Forgetting the diminutive in fixed expressions**

*een beet* ✗ → *een **beetje*** ✓ (*beetje* is always the diminutive form)

**3. Over-regularising to *-je***

*mannetje* (not *manje*) — short vowel + *n* triggers the *-netje* or *-etje* pattern with consonant doubling.

---

## Quick reference

```
Suffix:   -je  (most words)
          -tje (after l, r, n, or long vowel ending in consonant)
          -etje (short vowel + single consonant)
          -pje  (after -m clusters)

All diminutives → het  (always!)
Function:  small size, affection, informality, cultural warmth
```
