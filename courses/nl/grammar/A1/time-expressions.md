# Time expressions

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 13  
**Depends on:** [Present tense](present-tense.md)  
**Signal words:** *vandaag, morgen, gisteren, 's ochtends, om ... uur*

---

## Explanation

Time expressions (*tijdsbepalingen*) tell us **when** something happens. In Dutch they are extremely flexible: they can appear at the start of a sentence (triggering inversion), at the end, or embedded mid-clause. At A1 level, the most important building blocks are days, parts of the day, adverbs of time, and the clock.

---

## Days of the week

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| maandag | Monday |
| dinsdag | Tuesday |
| woensdag | Wednesday |
| donderdag | Thursday |
| vrijdag | Friday |
| zaterdag | Saturday |
| zondag | Sunday |

Days are written with a **lowercase** first letter in Dutch.

**"On Monday"** → *op maandag* or simply *maandag* (without preposition in informal speech):

> *Ik werk **op maandag**.* / *Ik werk **maandag**.*

---

## Parts of the day

| Dutch | English | Note |
|-------|---------|------|
| **'s ochtends** | in the morning | contraction of *des ochtends* |
| **'s middags** | in the afternoon | |
| **'s avonds** | in the evening | |
| **'s nachts** | at night | |
| **de ochtend** | the morning | used as a noun: *deze ochtend* |
| **de middag** | the afternoon | |
| **de avond** | the evening | |
| **de nacht** | the night | |

> *'s ochtends* etc. are adverbs (no article). *De ochtend* is a noun.

## Telling the time: **om ... uur**

Dutch clock time uses **om** (at) followed by a number and **uur** (o'clock):

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **om drie uur** | at three o'clock |
| **om half vier** | at half past three *(half = 30 min before the next hour!)* |
| **om kwart over twee** | at quarter past two |
| **om kwart voor vijf** | at quarter to five |
| **om tien over elf** | at ten past eleven |
| **om tien voor zeven** | at ten to seven |

> **Watch out:** *half vier* means 3:30, not 4:30. Dutch counts forward to the next hour: *half vier* = halfway to four.

**Asking the time:**  
*Hoe laat is het?* — What time is it?  
*Het is drie uur.* — It is three o'clock.

---

## Using time expressions with inversion

Time expressions at the start of a sentence cause **inversion** (verb before subject):

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Vandaag** werk ik thuis. | Today I work from home. |
| **'s Ochtends** drink ik koffie. | In the morning I drink coffee. |
| **Om acht uur** vertrekt de trein. | At eight o'clock the train departs. |
| **Morgen** gaan we naar de markt. | Tomorrow we go to the market. |

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Gisteren** was het koud. | Yesterday it was cold. |
| Ik slaap **'s nachts** goed. | I sleep well at night. |
| **Om kwart voor negen** begint de les. | The lesson starts at quarter to nine. |
| Ze werkt **altijd** hard. | She always works hard. |
| Hij is **net** thuis. | He just got home. |
| **Elke ochtend** eet hij brood. | Every morning he eats bread. |

## Common mistakes

**1. *half vier* = 3:30, not 4:30**

This catches English speakers every time. Always think: *half vier* = halfway *to* four.

**2. No inversion after mid-sentence time expressions**

*Ik werk vandaag thuis.* ✓ (time in middle, no inversion needed)  
***Vandaag** werk ik thuis.* ✓ (time first → inversion)

**3. Missing preposition *om* before clock times**

*De trein vertrekt drie uur.* ✗ → *De trein vertrekt **om** drie uur.* ✓

## Quick reference

```
Days:      op maandag / dinsdag / ...  (lowercase)
Parts:     's ochtends / 's middags / 's avonds / 's nachts
Clock:     om [number] uur  |  om half [next hour]  |  om kwart over/voor [hour]
Adverbs:   nu, vandaag, morgen, gisteren, altijd, nooit, soms, vaak, al, nog, net
```
