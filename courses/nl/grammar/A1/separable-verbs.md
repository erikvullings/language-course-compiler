# Separable verbs

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 17  
**Depends on:** [Main clause word order](word-order.md)

---

## Explanation

Many Dutch verbs consist of a **prefix + base verb**. Some of these prefixes are **separable**: in a main clause they **detach** from the verb and move to the **end** of the clause, while the conjugated base verb stays in position 2.

```
opstaan  (to get up) →  Ik sta om zeven uur op.
│  │                           │                │
prefix base                  base at pos.2  prefix at end
```

This is one of the most distinctively Dutch features of the language, and one of the most surprising for English speakers.

---

## Common separable verbs

| Separable verb | Meaning | Split form (ik) |
|---------------|---------|----------------|
| **opstaan** | to get up | ik sta **op** |
| **afwassen** | to do the dishes | ik was **af** |
| **aankomen** | to arrive | ik kom **aan** |
| **uitgaan** | to go out | ik ga **uit** |
| **thuiskomen** | to come home | ik kom **thuis** |
| **meenemen** | to take along | ik neem … **mee** |
| **bellen** → **opbellen** | to call (phone) | ik bel … **op** |
| **aantrekken** | to put on (clothes) | ik trek … **aan** |
| **uittrekken** | to take off (clothes) | ik trek … **uit** |
| **meedoen** | to join in / participate | ik doe **mee** |
| **vertellen** | to tell | ik vertel *(inseparable — prefix *ver-* is inseparable)* |

> **Tip:** common separable prefixes include *op-, af-, aan-, uit-, mee-, thuis-, in-, door-, terug-, neer-*. Prefixes like *ver-, be-, ge-, ont-, er-* are almost always inseparable.

---

## In a main clause: prefix moves to the end

| Full form | In a sentence |
|-----------|--------------|
| opstaan | Ik **sta** elke dag om zeven uur **op**. |
| afwassen | Hij **wast** na het eten **af**. |
| meenemen | Ze **neemt** haar boek **mee**. |
| thuiskomen | We **komen** laat **thuis**. |

With inversion (fronted adverb), the base verb stays in position 2 and the prefix remains at the end:

> *Om zeven uur **sta** ik **op**.* — At seven o'clock I get up.

---

## With modal verbs: prefix stays attached

When a separable verb is in **infinitive** position (after a modal or *gaan*), the verb is written as **one word** and nothing separates:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik moet **opstaan**. | I have to get up. |
| Ze wil **meegaan**. | She wants to come along. |
| We gaan **thuiskomen**. | We are going to come home. |

---

## In questions: same as statements, prefix at end

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Sta** jij altijd vroeg **op**? | Do you always get up early? |
| **Kom** je morgen **mee**? | Are you coming along tomorrow? |
| **Gaat** hij vanavond **uit**? | Is he going out this evening? |

---

## Negation: *niet* goes before the prefix

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik sta **niet** op. | I am not getting up. |
| Hij belt **niet** op. | He is not calling. |

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik **sta** elke ochtend om zes uur **op**. | I get up every morning at six o'clock. |
| Ze **trekt** haar jas **aan**. | She puts on her coat. |
| **Doe** jij ook **mee**? | Are you joining in too? |
| De trein **komt** om negen uur **aan**. | The train arrives at nine o'clock. |
| Hij **wast** elke avond **af**. | He does the dishes every evening. |
| Ik wil morgen **uitslapen**. | I want to sleep in tomorrow. |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Not separating in a main clause**

*Ik opstaan om zeven uur.* ✗ → *Ik sta om zeven uur **op**.* ✓

**2. Separating in infinitive position**

*Ik moet op staan.* ✗ → *Ik moet **opstaan**.* ✓

**3. Wrong position for the prefix**

The prefix always goes to the **absolute end** of the clause, after all other material:

*Ik sta op om zeven uur.* ✗ → *Ik sta om zeven uur **op**.* ✓

**4. Forgetting the prefix entirely**

*Ik sta elke ochtend.* — this sounds like *I stand every morning* (not *get up*). Include the prefix: *Ik sta elke ochtend **op**.*

---

## Quick reference

```
Main clause:      [Base verb at pos.2]  [middle]  [Prefix at end]
                  Ik sta  om zeven uur  op.

After modal/gaan: [infinitive stays together]
                  Ik moet opstaan.

Negation:         niet directly before the prefix
                  Ik sta niet op.
```
