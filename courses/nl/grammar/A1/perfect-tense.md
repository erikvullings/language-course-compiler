# Perfect tense

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 18  
**Depends on:** [zijn and hebben](zijn-en-hebben.md) | [Main clause word order](word-order.md)  
**Signal words:** *gisteren, vorige week, net, al*

---

## Explanation

The Dutch perfect tense (*voltooid tegenwoordige tijd*) is the main past tense in everyday speech and informal writing. It is used to talk about completed actions in the past — equivalent to both English *I have worked* and *I worked*.

```
[Subject]  [auxiliary]  [Rest]  [Past participle]
 Ik         heb          gisteren  gewerkt.
 I           have         yesterday worked.
```

It is formed with two elements:

1. A conjugated auxiliary verb: **hebben** or **zijn**
2. A **past participle** at the **end** of the clause

---

## Forming the past participle

### Regular verbs: **ge- + stem + d** or **ge- + stem + t**

1. Find the stem (infinitive minus *-en*)
2. Add **ge-** at the front
3. Add **-d** or **-t** at the end

| Ending | Rule | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| **-t** | if the last letter of the stem is in **'t kofschip** (*k, f, s, ch, p*) | werken → **ge**werk**t** |
| **-d** | all other stems | leven → **ge**leef**d** |

> **'t kofschip** (or **'t fokschaap**) is a mnemonic: if the stem's final consonant appears in this phrase, the past participle ends in *-t*; otherwise *-d*.

| Infinitive | Stem | Last letter in 't kofschip? | Participle |
|------------|------|-----------------------------|-----------|
| werken | werk | k ✓ | ge**werk**t |
| stoppen | stop | p ✓ | ge**stop**t |
| leven | leef | f ✓ | ge**leef**d *(voicing change: v→f in stem, but d ending)* |
| bellen | bel | l ✗ | ge**bel**d |
| wonen | woon | n ✗ | ge**woon**d |
| reizen | reis | s ✓ | ge**reis**d *(wait — s is in 't kofschip → t)* → ge**reis**t |

## Choosing the auxiliary: **hebben** or **zijn**

Most verbs take **hebben**. Use **zijn** for verbs that express:

- **motion** from one place to another: *gaan, komen, rijden, lopen, fietsen, vliegen, reizen*
- **change of state**: *worden (to become), vallen (to fall), sterven (to die), groeien (to grow)*
- A small group of fixed verbs: *zijn* itself (*ik ben geweest*), *blijven* (to stay → *gebleven*)

| Hebben | Zijn |
|--------|------|
| Ik heb gegeten. | Ik ben gegaan. |
| Ze heeft gewerkt. | Hij is gekomen. |
| We hebben gelezen. | Ze zijn gevallen. |

> When in doubt, use *hebben*. The *zijn* group is a learnable list, not a vast category.

---

## Word order

The auxiliary sits in **position 2** (finite verb). The past participle goes to the **end** of the clause:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik heb gisteren **gewerkt**. | I worked yesterday. |
| Ze heeft een boek **gelezen**. | She has read a book. |
| **Gisteren** ben ik naar Utrecht **gegaan**. | Yesterday I went to Utrecht. |

---

## Negation: *niet* before the participle

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik heb **niet** gewerkt. | I did not work. |
| Ze is **niet** gekomen. | She did not come. |

---

## Questions with the perfect

Move the auxiliary to position 1; participle stays at the end:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| **Heb** jij gegeten? | Have you eaten? / Did you eat? |
| **Is** hij al aangekomen? | Has he arrived yet? |

## Separable verbs in the perfect

The *ge-* prefix is inserted **between** the separable prefix and the base:

| Infinitive | Perfect | Note |
|------------|---------|------|
| opstaan | **op**ge**staan** | *ge-* between *op* and *staan* |
| afwassen | **af**ge**wassen** | |
| meenemen | **mee**ge**nomen** | |

> *Ik ben opgestaan.* — I got up. (*opstaan* uses *zijn*)

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Ik **heb** gisteren veel **gewerkt**. | I worked a lot yesterday. |
| Ze **heeft** een nieuwe fiets **gekocht**. | She bought a new bicycle. |
| Wij **zijn** vorige week naar Parijs **gegaan**. | We went to Paris last week. |
| **Heeft** hij al **gegeten**? | Has he eaten yet? |
| Ik **heb** het boek nog niet **gelezen**. | I have not read the book yet. |
| De kinderen **zijn** in de tuin **gespeeld**. | The children played in the garden. |
| Ze **heeft** hem **gebeld**. | She called him. |

## Common mistakes

**1. Putting the participle in position 2 (English habit)**

*Ik heb gewerkt gisteren.* ✗ → *Ik heb gisteren **gewerkt**.* ✓

**2. Wrong auxiliary (*hebben* instead of *zijn*)**

*Ik heb naar huis gegaan.* ✗ → *Ik **ben** naar huis **gegaan**.* ✓

**3. -d vs -t confusion**

*gewerkt* ✓ (*werk* ends in *k* → *'t kofschip* → *-t*)  
*geleefd* ✓ (*leef* → *f* is in *'t kofschip* — wait, is it *-t* or *-d*?)

> *f* is in *'t kofschip*, so the stem ending is *f* → participle is *-t*: *gelooft* (not *geloofd*). But the stem of *leven* is *leef* (*f* → kofschip → *t*): *geleefd* — actually this is irregular. Focus on the most common verbs first.

**4. Inseparable verbs get no *ge-* prefix**

Verbs with inseparable prefixes (*ver-, be-, ge-, ont-, er-*) do NOT add *ge-*:

*Ik heb **ver**teld.* (not *gever-teld*) — I told.  
*Ze heeft **be**taald.* — She paid.

---

## Quick reference

```
Structure:  [hebben / zijn (pos.2)]  [rest]  [ge- + stem + -d/t  (end)]

-t:  stem ends in k, f, s, ch, p  ('t kofschip)
-d:  all other stems

zijn:  motion (gaan, komen, rijden...), change of state (worden, vallen...)
hebben: everything else

Separable: op + ge + staan = opgestaan
Inseparable (ver-, be-, ge-, ont-, er-): no ge- prefix
```
