# Comparative and superlative

**CEFR level:** A1  
**Introduced in lesson:** 15  
**Depends on:** [Attributive adjectives](adjectives.md)

---

## Explanation

Dutch forms the comparative and superlative with suffixes added to the adjective. The system parallels English *-er* / *-est* but applies more regularly.

| Degree | Suffix | Example (groot) |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Positive (base) | — | groot (big) |
| Comparative | **-er** | groter (bigger) |
| Superlative | **-st** | grootst (biggest) |

---

## Forming the comparative: adjective + **-er**

Apply the same spelling rules as for other suffixes:

| Adjective | Comparative | Note |
|-----------|------------|------|
| groot | groot**er** | long *oo* stays *oo* before *-er* |
| klein | klein**er** | |
| oud | oud**er** | |
| dik | dik**ker** | short vowel + single consonant → double |
| lief | liev**er** | *f* → *v* before vowel suffix |
| mooi | mooier | |

---

## Forming the superlative: adjective + **-st** / **-ste**

| Adjective | Superlative (predicative) | Superlative (attributive) |
|-----------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| groot | het grootst | de/het grootst**e** |
| klein | het kleinst | de/het kleinst**e** |
| oud | het oudst | de/het oudst**e** |
| dik | het dikst | de/het dikst**e** |

The predicative superlative is usually preceded by **het**, even when referring to a *de*-word noun:

> *Deze fiets is het **snel**ste.* — This bicycle is the fastest.  
> *Hij is de **oudste** in de klas.* — He is the oldest in the class. *(attributive, with article)*

---

## Comparative sentences: **dan** (than)

Use **dan** to make a comparison:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Mijn fiets is groot**er** dan jouw fiets. | My bicycle is bigger than yours. |
| Zij is oud**er** dan hij. | She is older than he is. |
| Dit boek is interessant**er** dan dat boek. | This book is more interesting than that book. |

---

## Equality: **even ... als** (as ... as)

To say two things are equal, use **even ... als**:

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Mijn huis is **even groot als** jouw huis. | My house is as big as yours. |
| Hij loopt **even snel als** zijn broer. | He walks as fast as his brother. |

---

## Irregular comparatives and superlatives

A handful of very common adjectives are irregular:

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| **goed** (good) | **beter** | **best** |
| **veel** (much/many) | **meer** | **meest** |
| **weinig** (little/few) | **minder** | **minst** |
| **graag** (gladly) | **liever** | **liefst** |

> *graag / liever / liefst* express preference: *Ik drink **liever** thee dan koffie.* — I prefer tea to coffee.

---

## Examples

| Dutch | English |
|-------|---------|
| Amsterdam is **groter** dan Utrecht. | Amsterdam is bigger than Utrecht. |
| Dit is het **beste** restaurant in de stad. | This is the best restaurant in the city. |
| Ze is de **jongste** in haar familie. | She is the youngest in her family. |
| Hij werkt **harder** dan zijn collega. | He works harder than his colleague. |
| Ik drink **liever** water dan frisdrank. | I prefer water to soft drinks. |
| Welke fiets is het **goedkoopst**? | Which bicycle is the cheapest? |

---

## Common mistakes

**1. Using *meer* for regular adjectives**

English uses *more* before long adjectives. Dutch uses *-er* for virtually all adjectives:

*meer interessant* ✗ → *interessant**er*** ✓  
*meer mooi* ✗ → *mooier* ✓

**2. Double consonant in comparative**

*dikker* (not *diker*) — the short-vowel spelling rule applies.

**3. Forgetting *het* before predicative superlatives**

*Dit boek is interessantste.* ✗ → *Dit boek is **het** interessantste.* ✓

**4. Using *als* instead of *dan* in comparatives**

*Hij is groter als ik.* ✗ → *Hij is groter **dan** ik.* ✓  
(*als* is for equality: *even groot **als***)

---

## Quick reference

```
Comparative:   adjective + -er  (+ dan for than)
Superlative:   adjective + -st  (het + adj + -ste for predicative)
Equality:      even + adjective + als

Irregulars:  goed → beter → best
             veel → meer → meest
             weinig → minder → minst
             graag → liever → liefst
```
